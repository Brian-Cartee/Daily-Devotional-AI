## Prompt

We've done this kind of UX/copy review together before using mobile screenshots of this same app, so you may have some familiarity with it. We're now doing a fresh round using full browser captures of the current build.

**The app:** Shepherd's Path — a Christian faith app whose core mission is guiding users toward a personal relationship with Jesus. Not a Bible reference tool. Not a habit tracker. A companion for the spiritual journey.

**Current phase:** UX and copy polish. We are not adding features. We are deepening the emotional quality of the experience — making every screen feel like an invitation, not a product.

---

### VOICE RULES (apply these to your review)

- No theological jargon
- No "AI" mentioned anywhere in the UI
- No habit/streak pressure language
- Capitalize pronouns only when they unmistakably refer to God, Jesus, or the Holy Spirit — not general spiritual references
- Every screen should feel like: *"There's something here for me"* — not *"I'm using an app"*

### WHAT WE WANT FROM YOU

Please review each screen and give us:

1. **Copy audit** — Any line that reads like an app, a product, a program, or a feature instead of a companion. Flag it and suggest a rewrite.

2. **Emotional temperature check** — Does each screen feel warm, safe, and inviting? Or does anything feel transactional, pressuring, or distant? Be specific.

3. **First-time user gut check** — Looking at the primary flow (screens 1–4): does this feel like something a spiritually curious but skeptical person would stay with, or does something push them out?

4. **One thing to fix first** — If you could only tell us one thing to change right now, what would it be and why?

---

### WHAT WE ARE NOT ASKING FOR

- Do not suggest new features
- Do not comment on visual design choices (colors, layout, typography)
- Do not suggest adding gamification, streaks, or progress indicators
- Do not suggest making the app "more social"

Focus entirely on: **Does the copy and the emotional experience match the mission?**
