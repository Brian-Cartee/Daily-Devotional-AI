We are refining the "Study" experience in Shepherd’s Path.

This is not a search engine.
This is not a research tool.

This is:
a place where someone comes to understand something they’re carrying — through scripture.

-----------------------------------
CORE EXPERIENCE PRINCIPLE
-----------------------------------

The Study experience should feel like:

- being guided into understanding
- being helped, not searching
- something unfolding, not retrieved
- scripture meeting the person

NOT:

- typing a query and getting results
- a Bible Google
- a knowledge lookup tool

-----------------------------------
HEADER + POSITIONING
-----------------------------------

Current:
“Quick Bible Study”
“Any topic, passage, or question — studied in seconds.”

Problem:
Too fast, too mechanical, too transactional.

Refinement:

Option 1:
“Explore Scripture”

Subtext:
“Bring a question, a passage, or something on your mind.”

Option 2:
“Find understanding in scripture”

Subtext:
“Whatever you’re thinking about — start here.”

Key principle:
Slow it down. Remove “seconds.” Remove speed emphasis.

-----------------------------------
SEARCH INPUT (MOST IMPORTANT SHIFT)
-----------------------------------

Current placeholder:
e.g. “anxiety”, “Romans 8”, “forgiveness”

Problem:
Feels like search keywords.

Refinement:

Placeholder:

“What’s on your mind?”

OR

“What are you trying to understand?”

OR

“Bring a question, a verse, or a situation…”

-----------------------------------

BUTTON

Current:
“Study”

Refinement options:

“Explore”
“Start here”
“See what scripture says”

Key principle:
Avoid command tone.

-----------------------------------
SUGGESTED TOPICS (CHIPS)
-----------------------------------

Current:
anxiety, forgiveness, Romans 8, etc.

These are good — but feel like filters.

Refinement:

Keep them, but introduce with:

“Start with something familiar…”

OR

“Some places people begin…”

Key principle:
Make them feel like gentle entry points, not categories.

-----------------------------------
READING TRACKS
-----------------------------------

Current:
“Choose your track”

Problem:
Feels like a program or course.

Refinement:

“Follow a path”

OR

“Start somewhere”

-----------------------------------

CARD COPY REFINEMENT

The Songs:

The Way:

The Good News:

-----------------------------------
“FIND A PASSAGE”
-----------------------------------

Current:
“Story, verse, or just a memory — we’ll find it”

This is close, but slightly system-like.

Refinement:

“Looking for something specific?”

Subtext:
“A verse, a moment, or something you remember…”

Key principle:
Make it feel optional, not primary.

-----------------------------------
EMAIL CAPTURE (IMPORTANT)
-----------------------------------

Current:
“Get today’s verse delivered to you”

This is fine — but feels like a marketing block inside a sacred flow.

Refinement:

“Receive the daily verse”

Subtext:
“A quiet reminder each morning.”

CTA:
“Send it to me”

Key principle:
Invitation, not subscription energy.

-----------------------------------
OVERALL FLOW SHIFT
-----------------------------------

Right now:
Search → Results → Consume

We want:
Prompt → Reflection → Understanding

-----------------------------------

IMPORTANT UX SHIFT:

After user submits input:

DO NOT immediately show results like a search engine.

Instead:

1. Reflect their input back:
“You’re thinking about anxiety…”

2. Introduce scripture:
“There are a few places this shows up…”

3. Then guide:
“Let’s start here…”

-----------------------------------

This aligns Study with Guidance.

-----------------------------------
GLOBAL TONE RULES
-----------------------------------

1. NO SPEED LANGUAGE
- remove “quick”
- remove “seconds”

2. NO SEARCH ENGINE FEEL
- avoid keywords
- avoid “results”

3. INVITATION OVER ACTION
- “start”
- “explore”
- “see”

NOT:
- “submit”
- “search”
- “generate”

4. CONSISTENT VOICE
Must match:
- Devotional
- Guidance
- Journal

Feels like same presence.

-----------------------------------
FINAL STANDARD

Ask:

Does this feel like:
“I’m searching for information”

Or:
“I’m being guided into understanding”

If it feels like search → rewrite.
If it feels like guidance → keep.
