## **Shepherd’s Path — Product Direction (Q6–Q8)**

---

### **Question 6 — Onboarding (First 3 Minutes)**

**Goal:**
Make the user feel something real — immediately — without explaining anything.

---

### **What it should NOT be:**

* No tutorial
* No walkthrough
* No setup friction
* No “enter your name to begin”

---

### **What it SHOULD feel like:**

> *“I didn’t expect this… but this is for me.”*

---

### **First 3-Minute Experience (Ideal Flow):**

**Minute 0–1 — Arrival**

* Verse is present
* Calm, uncluttered
* No instructions

👉 The user should feel:

> “I can just be here”

---

**Minute 1–2 — First Engagement**

* User taps, listens, or pauses
* Reflection begins

👉 This is the moment that matters most:

> The reflection must feel *personally relevant*

---

**Minute 2–3 — Connection**

* Prayer follows naturally
* No pressure to complete anything

👉 The user should feel:

> “This understood me”

---

### **What the user must feel to return:**

* Seen (not generic)
* Safe (no pressure, no expectations)
* Curious (there’s more here)

---

### **Subtle addition (optional):**

After first session, gently ask for name:

> “What should we call you?”

Only after value is felt.

---

### **Core Principle:**

> Don’t onboard the user — let the experience speak for itself

---

---

### **Question 7 — Social Sharing**

**Core truth:**
People don’t share faith content to promote something.

They share because:

> **something meant something to them — or reminded them of someone**

---

### **What makes sharing happen:**

#### 1. Personal resonance

> “This spoke to me”

#### 2. Relational trigger

> “This made me think of you”

---

### **What sharing should feel like:**

* Personal
* Intentional
* Almost private, even when public

---

### **What it should NOT feel like:**

* Marketing
* Broadcasting
* “Check out this app”

---

### **Best framing for sharing:**

Not:

> “Share this”

But:

> **“Did someone come to mind?”**

---

### **Key design principle:**

Sharing should feel like:

> passing something meaningful along

Not:

> posting content

---

### **The one thing it must never feel like:**

> **Promotion**

---

---

### **Question 8 — Long-Term Habit Formation (Day 30+)**

**Early stage (Day 1–7):**

* Driven by emotional impact
* “This helped me”

---

### **Long-term stage (Day 30+):**

Retention shifts from:

> emotional moment

To:

> **spiritual rhythm**

---

### **What the app must become:**

Not:

> something they check

But:

> **a place they return to**

---

### **What it should feel like:**

* Familiar
* Grounding
* Personal
* Steady

---

### **Key shift:**

Early:

> “This is powerful”

Later:

> **“This is part of my life”**

---

### **What drives long-term retention:**

#### 1. Continuity

User feels:

> “This is connected to my journey”

---

#### 2. Identity

User feels:

> “This is something I do”

---

#### 3. Simplicity

No friction
No thinking required

---

---

### **What changes after Day 30:**

Less need for:

* novelty
* explanation

More need for:

* consistency
* reliability
* depth

---

---

### **Core Principle:**

> Habits form when something becomes part of who someone is — not just something they use

---

---

## **Final Summary**

**Onboarding:**

* Let the experience speak
* No instructions, no friction

---

**Sharing:**

* Driven by meaning and relationships
* Never feels like promotion

---

**Long-Term Retention:**

* Shift from emotional moments → spiritual rhythm
* Become part of the user’s life

---

## **End Goal**

* The app feels personal from day one
* Sharing feels natural, not forced
* Long-term use feels like returning, not checking
