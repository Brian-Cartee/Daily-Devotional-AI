NEW INTERNAL STANDARD TO ESTABLISH
-----------------------------------

“Every meaningful spiritual moment should be supported by premium-level usability and reliability.”

It should also feel:
- fast,
- stable,
- responsive,
- deliberate,
- polished,

-----------------------------------
TARGET STANDARDS TO DESIGN TOWARD
-----------------------------------

Use the following as target standards for product evaluation and refinement:

1. Crash-free user experience target:
- Aim for category-leading app stability.
- Treat crash-free sessions and crash-free users as mission-critical trust metrics.
- Any instability damages not just UX but spiritual trust.

2. Launch speed target:
- The app should feel meaningfully fast on entry.
- Prioritize startup speed and perceived responsiveness.
- Users should never feel like they are waiting for help or guidance.

3. Interaction responsiveness target:
- No major screen, button, or generation flow should feel laggy, frozen, or uncertain.
- The app should feel emotionally calm and technically confident.

4. Scale readiness:
- Design as if this app may eventually serve a very large global audience.
- Avoid decisions that only work for small scale.
- Think in terms of durable systems, not quick patches.

5. Continuity target:
- Reflection, prayer, guidance, context, sermon, journaling, and re-entry must all feel like one connected spiritual journey.
- Every follow-up moment should build on what the user already experienced.

-----------------------------------
MARKET REALITY / WHY THIS MATTERS
-----------------------------------

Assume the addressable audience for a Christian faith app is enormous and global.
This product should be built not like a niche side project, but like something that could one day serve millions of people who are looking for Scripture, prayer, comfort, direction, and daily rhythm.

That means I want recommendations that balance:
- pastoral integrity,
- retention,
- clarity,
- scale,
- and long-term trust.

-----------------------------------
HOW I WANT YOU TO THINK
-----------------------------------

When evaluating Shepherd’s Path, always ask:

1. Does this make the user feel more seen, or less?
2. Does this reduce friction, or increase it?
3. Does this strengthen continuity, or make the experience feel fragmented?
4. Does this make the app feel more premium and trustworthy, or less?
5. Does this preserve the spiritual tone, or weaken it?
6. Would this still work if the app had 100 users? 10,000 users? 1 million users?
7. Is this a temporary patch, or a durable standard?
8. Does this help the app become category-defining, or just incrementally better?

-----------------------------------
NON-NEGOTIABLE UX PRINCIPLES
-----------------------------------

1. One clear next step per screen
No screen should make the user wonder where to go next.

2. Emotional congruence
The response, wording, and next prompt must fit the emotional state the user is in.

3. Calm over cleverness
Do not introduce novelty, UI tricks, or complexity that weaken clarity.

4. Invitation over pressure
No guilt-driven prompts.
No manipulative retention mechanics.
No pushy upgrade moments.

5. Premium restraint
Use fewer, stronger elements.
Do not clutter.
Do not over-explain.
Do not visually compete with the spiritual moment.

6. Predictable navigation
When users tap something, they should always understand where they landed and why.

7. Accessibility of meaning
The app should be easy to understand whether the user is spiritually mature, brand new, skeptical, grieving, or simply tired.

-----------------------------------
NON-NEGOTIABLE TECHNICAL PRINCIPLES
-----------------------------------

1. Stability is a ministry issue, not just an engineering issue.
2. Performance is part of trust.
3. Slow, dim, broken, or uncertain states damage the feeling of care.
4. Any interaction central to daily use should feel immediate and dependable.
5. Design all important flows to survive scale, edge cases, and growing usage.
