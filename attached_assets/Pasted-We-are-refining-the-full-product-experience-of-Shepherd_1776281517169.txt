We are refining the full product experience of Shepherd’s Path.

This is not a feature-by-feature optimization.

This is a unification of voice, tone, and emotional experience across the entire app.

---

## CORE PRODUCT IDENTITY

Shepherd’s Path is not:

* a Bible app
* a content platform
* a productivity tool
* a self-help system

Shepherd’s Path is:

a quiet, consistent presence that meets someone where they are and gently walks with them toward truth.

---

## THE EXPERIENCE STANDARD

Every screen, message, and interaction must feel like:

* calm, not urgent
* present, not instructive
* personal, not generalized
* invitational, not directive

The app should never feel like it is:

* telling the user what to do
* explaining itself
* asking for effort
* requiring decisions

Instead, it should feel like:

“you can begin here”

---

## THE FIVE CORE MODES (UNIFIED)

The app has five functional areas:

1. Devotional
2. Guidance (Talk It Through)
3. Study
4. Journeys
5. Journal

They must NOT feel like separate tools.

They must feel like:

different moments within the same relationship.

---

## MODE DEFINITIONS

Devotional:
A daily anchor — something received, not chosen.

Guidance:
A conversation — something expressed and responded to.

Study:
Exploration — something understood, not searched.

Journeys:
Continuation — something walked, not selected.

Journal:
Reflection — something remembered, not stored.

---

## CRITICAL RULE — NO MODE BREAKS

At no point should the user feel like they are:

* switching tools
* entering a new system
* starting over

Transitions must feel like:

a natural continuation of the same moment.

---

## VOICE & LANGUAGE RULES

1. REMOVE ALL SYSTEM LANGUAGE

Avoid:

* “saved”
* “no items yet”
* “your data”
* “this feature”
* “how it works”

Replace with:
language that reflects meaning, not mechanics.

---

2. REMOVE ALL PRODUCT LANGUAGE

Avoid:

* “plans”
* “tracks”
* “program”
* “content”
* “library”

Replace with:

* path
* moment
* place
* step
* beginning

---

3. REMOVE ALL URGENCY

Avoid:

* “start now”
* “don’t miss”
* “quick”
* “in seconds”

Replace with:

* “begin”
* “when you’re ready”
* silence (no pressure language at all)

---

4. SPEAK TO THE PERSON, NOT THE FEATURE

Every piece of copy should reflect:

* what the user may be feeling
* what they may be carrying
* what they may be wondering

NOT:

* what the feature does

---

5. QUIET CONFIDENCE

The app should feel:

* grounded
* assured
* not trying to impress
* not trying to convert

---

## THE INVISIBLE HANDOFF (MOST IMPORTANT)

The app must feel like a single continuous experience across:

Guidance → Devotional → Study → Journeys → Journal

Examples:

If a user shares something in Guidance:
→ Devotional should feel relevant, not random

If a user reads something meaningful:
→ Journal should feel like a natural place to hold it

If a user is exploring in Study:
→ Journeys should feel like a next step, not a separate decision

---

There should never be a moment where the user thinks:

“What should I do next?”

The app should gently make that obvious without stating it directly.

---

## EMPTY STATE STANDARD

Empty states must NEVER feel like:

* absence
* failure
* inactivity

They should feel like:

* something that will become meaningful
* a place waiting to be filled over time

---

## ENGAGEMENT PRINCIPLE

We are not optimizing for:

* clicks
* time spent
* feature usage

We are optimizing for:

* return
* trust
* emotional connection

---

## FINAL EVALUATION TEST

For every screen, ask:

Does this feel like:

“I am using an app”

OR:

“I am returning to something familiar”

If it feels like an app → revise
If it feels like a place → keep

---

## TASK

Review the Shepherd’s Path experience holistically.

Identify:

1. Where the experience breaks (feels like a tool, feature, or system)
2. Where language becomes mechanical or product-driven
3. Where transitions feel like switching instead of continuing
4. Where decisions are being asked instead of invitations being made

Then provide:

* Specific copy refinements
* Structural adjustments (only if they support emotional flow)
* Suggestions to strengthen continuity between sections

Do NOT suggest:

* adding features
* increasing complexity
* gamification or metrics

All recommendations must:

protect simplicity, deepen meaning, and preserve the core identity.
