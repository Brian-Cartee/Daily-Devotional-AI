## **Shepherd’s Path — Product Direction (Q12–Q14)**

---

### **Question 12 — The Long Absence**

**Core truth:**
This is not a retention moment.
This is a **pastoral moment**.

---

### **What the user may feel:**

* Guilt (“I fell off”)
* Distance (“I’ve been away”)
* Pain (“Something happened”)

---

### **What the app must NOT do:**

* Do not acknowledge absence directly (“It’s been 14 days…”)
* Do not reference streaks or missed time
* Do not try to “bring them back”

---

### **What it SHOULD feel like:**

> **“You’re still welcome here.”**

---

### **Best approach:**

#### Option A — Silence (often best)

* No special message
* Just the verse
* Calm, unchanged

👉 This communicates:

> Nothing has changed. You can step back in.

---

#### Option B — Soft, non-specific welcome (used sparingly)

> “I’m really glad you’re here.”
>
> “You can start right where you are.”

---

👉 No mention of time away
👉 No acknowledgment of absence

---

### **Core Principle:**

> Remove shame, remove pressure, remove memory of absence

---

---

### **Question 13 — Push Notifications**

**Core truth:**
A notification is not a reminder.

👉 It is an **interruption into someone’s life**

---

### **What makes a notification worth sending:**

It must feel like:

> **a gentle nudge, not a demand**

---

### **What it SHOULD feel like:**

* Calm
* Timely
* Personal (even if broadly sent)
* Easy to ignore without guilt

---

### **What it must NOT feel like:**

* Obligation
* Pressure
* Repetition
* Noise

---

### **Examples of correct tone:**

* “A moment when you’re ready.”
* “Today’s verse is here.”
* “You don’t have to carry everything alone.”

---

### **Frequency guidance:**

* 1 per day max
* Optional opt-in timing (morning, evening)
* No stacking or catch-up notifications

---

### **The version that deepens practice:**

Not:

> “Open the app”

But:

> **A moment that can stand on its own**

---

👉 Even if they don’t tap it:

* it still speaks
* it still gives something

---

### **Core Principle:**

> A notification should give, not ask

---

---

### **Question 14 — What the App Should Never Become**

**Core truth:**
Your strength is restraint.

---

### **The traps:**

#### 1. Becoming a content platform

* Feeds
* Endless scrolling
* “More to explore”

👉 Breaks focus

---

#### 2. Becoming a productivity tool

* Streak pressure
* Completion metrics
* Habit tracking dominance

👉 Shifts from presence → performance

---

#### 3. Becoming a social network

* Likes
* Comments
* Public validation

👉 Turns inward faith → outward performance

---

#### 4. Becoming feature-heavy

* Too many entry points
* Too many options
* Too many paths

👉 Creates confusion instead of clarity

---

#### 5. Becoming authoritative

* Acting as the primary spiritual voice
* Replacing real community or leadership

👉 Breaks trust and responsibility

---

---

### **What “too much” looks like:**

* The user has to think about what to do
* The user scrolls instead of enters
* The experience feels busy instead of calm
* The app explains itself instead of being felt

---

---

### **What the app must protect at all costs:**

* Simplicity
* Continuity
* Emotional safety
* Spiritual humility

---

---

### **What to explicitly refuse to build:**

* Infinite content feeds
* Engagement mechanics (likes, comments, rankings)
* Gamified spirituality (points, badges, pressure loops)
* Complex navigation structures
* Anything that competes with the devotional flow

---

---

### **Core Principle:**

> If it adds noise, choice, or pressure — it does not belong

---

---

## **Final Summary**

**Long Absence:**

* No shame, no tracking, no reminders
* Welcome without memory

---

**Push Notifications:**

* Gentle, optional, meaningful
* Give something even if unopened

---

**Product Boundaries:**

* Stay simple
* Stay focused
* Resist expansion that adds noise

---

## **End Goal**

* The app always feels safe to return to
* Every interaction feels intentional
* Growth never compromises the core experience
