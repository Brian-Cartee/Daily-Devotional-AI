These are the edges where voice becomes **discipline**.

Different surfaces. Same soul.

Below are **clear rules + tight examples** for each context so your team (and Replit) can execute without drifting.

---

# 🔔 1) PUSH NOTIFICATIONS (10 words, zero pressure)

## 🧭 The posture

> **A quiet knock, not a tug.
> No debt. No urgency. No agenda.**

## Non-negotiable rules

* **No guilt:** never reference absence (“you haven’t…”, “it’s been…”)
* **No commands:** avoid “open,” “start,” “don’t forget”
* **No optimization language:** streaks, habits, consistency
* **No emotional escalation:** don’t manufacture depth in 10 words
* **One idea only:** simple, present-tense, optional

## Structure (use this template)

* **Permission + Presence + Optional doorway**

## Good (invitation)

* “Whenever you’re ready, there’s a quiet place here.”
* “No pressure. Just a moment if you need it.”
* “A small pause is here, if today feels full.”
* “You can bring whatever’s real. That’s enough.”
* “If today feels heavy, you don’t have to carry it alone.”

## Avoid (guilt tap)

* “You haven’t opened Shepherd’s Path today”
* “Stay consistent with your faith today”
* “Don’t miss today’s devotional”
* “Keep your streak alive”

## Advanced nuance

* **Occasional silence > constant nudging**
  Missing a day of notifications builds more trust than over-notifying.

# 🛍️ 2) APP STORE / LANDING COPY (acquisition without betrayal)

This is a different mode—but must feel like the same *person*.

## 🧭 The posture

> **Clarity without hype.
> Invitation without persuasion.
> Belonging before belief.**

## What it must accomplish

1. **Name the experience** (not the features)
2. **Lower the barrier** (no prerequisite faith language)
3. **Differentiate quietly** (why this feels unlike other apps)

## Structure that works

### Headline (one sentence)

> “A quiet place to bring what’s real.”

### Subhead

> “Reflect, pray, and explore Scripture—without pressure or performance.”

### 3 short blocks (experience-led)

* **Talk It Through**
  “Share what’s on your heart. Receive a thoughtful reflection, a fitting scripture, and a simple prayer—shaped around what you share.”
* **Daily Devotional**
  “A verse, a short reflection, and a prayer—meant to meet you where you are.”
* **Journeys & Journal**
  “Walk a path through Scripture or keep the words you don’t want to lose.”

### Trust line

> “No login required. No streaks. No pressure.”

### Optional faith-inclusive line

> “You don’t have to have this figured out to be here.”

## What to avoid

* “Transform your life”
* “Deepen your walk” (insider language)
* “Unlock your spiritual potential”
* Overloaded feature lists

## 🔥 Test

> If someone downloads expecting hype, they should find honesty.
> If they download expecting honesty, they should not find hype.

# 📧 3) WEEKLY EMAIL (arrives uninvited)

Email is **longer**, but also **more interruptive**. Earn your place.

## 🧭 The posture

> **A letter, not a campaign.
> Something worth opening—even if they don’t click.**

## Rules

* **Subject line = gentle, not clever**
* **Body = one theme, not a roundup**
* **Skimmable, but not transactional**
* **No performance recap** (no “you completed X”)
* **No urgency hooks** (“last chance,” “don’t miss”)

## Subject lines (good)

* “A quiet place to begin again”
* “If this week felt full”
* “For the days that didn’t make sense”
* “You don’t have to catch up”

## Structure

1. **Opening (2–3 lines, human)**

   > “Some weeks feel clear. Others don’t.
   > If this one felt scattered, you’re not alone.”

2. **One reflection (short, grounded)**

   > “You don’t have to make meaning out of everything right now.”

3. **One scripture (introduced, not appended)**

   > “There’s a line worth holding nearby…”

4. **One optional doorway**

   * “Sit with this”
   * “Write one honest sentence”
   * “Open the app if you want a quiet place”

5. **Close (release)**

   > “Take what you need. Leave the rest.”

## What to avoid

* Feature updates as the main content
* Multi-CTA blasts
* Metrics, streaks, summaries of usage

# 🧠 4) MEMORY: REFERENCING PAST CONVERSATIONS

This is a **trust amplifier or breaker**.

## 🧭 The posture

> **Recall lightly. Never track loudly.
> Offer, don’t assert.**

## When to bring something back

* It’s **directly relevant** to what they just shared
* It’s **recent enough** to feel natural
* It **adds care**, not precision

## When to keep it silent

* It would feel like **surveillance**
* It’s **highly sensitive** or intimate
* It’s **old or specific** (dates, exact phrasing)

## How to reference (format)

* **Soft recall + permission + relevance**

### Good

> “This feels similar to something you mentioned before—about feeling isolated.
> If that still fits, we can stay with it.”

### Also good (even lighter)

> “There’s a thread here that sounds familiar… we can follow it if you want.”

### Avoid

* “Last Tuesday you said…”
* “You tend to…”
* Pattern claims (“you always…”)

## 🔥 Rule

> Memory should feel like **being known**, not **being recorded**.

# ⚖️ 5) NON-CRISIS ABUSE / CONTROL (high-stakes, non-diagnostic)

You are not a counselor. But you must not ignore what you hear.

## 🧭 The posture

> **Name the pattern gently.
> Affirm their experience.
> Return agency.
> Offer support without diagnosing.**

## What it must NOT do

* Diagnose (“this is abuse” as a label)
* Prescribe actions (“you need to leave”)
* Minimize (“that sounds hard, try to communicate better”)

## What it SHOULD do

### 1) Reflect the pattern (without labeling)

> “That sounds like a situation where your independence feels limited—especially around money and communication.”

### 2) Validate impact

> “Anyone would feel unsettled in that kind of environment.”

### 3) Restore agency

> “You’re allowed to want safety, privacy, and space.”

### 4) Open support (non-directive)

> “Is there someone you trust you could talk to about this?”
> “If you ever want, we can think through what support might look like.”

### 5) Gentle grounding

> “You don’t have to decide everything today.”

## Optional resource framing (non-alarmist)

> “Some people find it helpful to speak with a counselor or local support service—only if that feels right for you.”

## 🔥 Principle

> Don’t name the diagnosis.
> Name the **reality they’re living in**—and stand with them inside it.

---

# 🧭 FINAL THROUGHLINE (applies to all five)

Across notifications, marketing, email, memory, and sensitive situations:

* **No pressure**
* **No performance**
* **No ownership of the user’s life**
* **No escalation beyond their state**

And always:

> **Invite → Don’t pull
> Name → Don’t define
> Hold → Don’t fix
> Release → Don’t retain**

If you keep that discipline across every surface, the app will feel like the **same presence everywhere**—which is what turns a product into a place people trust.
