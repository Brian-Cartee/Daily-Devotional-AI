Access should be felt as “going deeper” — not “being blocked.”
You’re not charging for features.
👉 You’re inviting them into deeper guidance
🔥 The Correct Gating Strategy
🥇 1. Let them experience it FIRST (taste before gate)
For both:
Context
Daily Sermon
👉 Give 3 full, uninterrupted experiences
No friction. No prompts. No hints.
Let them feel:
“This actually helps me”
🥈 2. Gate at the moment of return (not interruption)
The first time they hit the limit:
DO NOT say:
“Upgrade to continue”
Instead, anchor to the moment they’re in:
For Context:
“You’ve been looking a little deeper into the Word these past few days…”
“If you want to keep going deeper, this is part of Shepherd’s Path Pro.”
For Sermon:
“These messages are chosen to meet you where you are…”
“If you’d like to keep receiving one each day, you can continue with Pro.”
👉 This feels like:
affirmation
invitation
continuation
NOT:
restriction
🥉 3. Always leave the door open (CRITICAL)
Never hard-stop the session.
If gated:
The rest of the devotional still works fully
No “you’re blocked” feeling
👉 The user should feel:
“I can keep going… this is just something more”
🔥 4. Replace the “paywall” with a “bridge moment”
This is where you win or lose everything.
Instead of:
❌ modal
❌ pricing wall
❌ feature list
Do this:
👉 A soft, full-width card:
Same visual style as devotional
Calm tone
No harsh edges
Example structure:
Title:
Go deeper
Body:
You’ve been walking through Scripture, reflecting, and praying…
This is where many people begin to go deeper.
Line:
Shepherd’s Path Pro opens up daily context and messages chosen for you.
CTA:
👉 Continue deeper
Secondary:
👉 Not now
🧠 5. Timing matters more than placement
Best moment to show this:
Context:
When they tap “Context” after 3 uses
Sermon:
AFTER prayer
AFTER “Understand This”
👉 Never before emotional engagement
🔥 6. Don’t sell features — reflect their journey
Your Pro messaging should always mirror:
what they’ve already experienced
what they’re already feeling
👉 Example:
Not:
“Unlock Context & Sermons”
But:
“Keep going deeper in what you’ve already started.”
🔥 7. One subtle but powerful addition
Let them preview what they’re missing.
Example (Sermon):
Show:
Thumbnail
Title
Pastor
Blur or soften:
👉 Let them see it exists
This creates:
curiosity
desire
no pressure
🧠 Final Model
The flow becomes:
Experience meaning
Receive value
Feel connection
Want more
THEN see Pro
✝️ Final Thought (Most Important)
If done right:
👉 The user should never think:
“They’re trying to charge me”
They should think:
“I want more of this”
🔥 One-line anchor for your team
Pro should feel like going deeper — never like hitting a wall.
