# ✍️ 1. “What are you carrying?” — Talk It Through

## 🌅 Morning versions (gentle, grounding)

* “What are you carrying into today?”
* “What’s already on your mind this morning?”
* “Before the day gets going… what’s sitting with you?”
* “What feels present as today begins?”
* “Is there anything you’re holding before you step into today?”

---

## 🌙 Night versions (reflective, exhale)

* “What are you still carrying from today?”
* “What didn’t leave you when the day ended?”
* “Before you rest… what’s still on your heart?”
* “What feels unresolved tonight?”
* “What are you bringing with you into rest?”

---

## 🔁 After absence (grace-centered, no guilt)

* “You’re here. What’s been on your heart lately?”
* “No need to catch up. What’s with you right now?”
* “Whenever you’re ready… what have you been carrying?”
* “It’s okay to start here. What’s been weighing on you?”
* “We can pick up right here. What’s been sitting with you?”

---

## 🔥 Strongest single default (if you had to pick one):

> “What are you carrying that you haven’t said out loud yet?”

---

# 📖 2. Prayer Journal — Empty State

## First-time (clean, inviting)

* “This is where your prayers will live.”
* “Nothing here yet — just space for what matters.”
* “You don’t need perfect words. Just start where you are.”
* “This space is yours. Honest is enough.”
* “When you’re ready, write your first prayer.”

---

## Slightly more relational (my favorite direction)

> “Some prayers are meant to be remembered.
> This is where they’ll stay.”

---

## With soft guidance:

* “Start with one sentence. That’s enough.”
* “You can write it exactly as you feel it.”

# ⏳ 5. AI “Thinking / Loading” States

This is one of your biggest opportunities to differentiate.

Most apps say:

> “Thinking…”

You should feel like:

> presence, not processing

---

## 🧠 DO NOT:

* “Analyzing…”
* “Generating response…”
* “Processing…”

---

## ✅ Use these instead:

### Grounded presence:

* “Give me a moment to sit with this…”
* “I’m here with you…”
* “Let’s take a breath before we continue…”

---

### Slightly more active:

* “Listening closely…”
* “Holding this for a moment…”
* “Taking this in…”

---

### With subtle movement (VERY strong):

> “Stay here a moment…”

---

### If slightly longer delay:

> “This deserves a thoughtful response…”
