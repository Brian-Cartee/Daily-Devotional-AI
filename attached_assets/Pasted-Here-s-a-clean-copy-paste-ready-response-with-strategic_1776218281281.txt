## **Shepherd’s Path — Product Direction (Q3–Q5)**

---

### **Question 3 — Progressive Reveal**

**Goal:**
Create a guided experience without making it feel controlled or forced.

---

### **What it should NOT be:**

* Not tap-to-unlock steps
* Not gated progression
* Not “complete this to continue”

---

### **What it SHOULD feel like:**

> The next step naturally *appears* as the user leans in

---

### **Recommended Model: Passive + Responsive Reveal**

**Step 1 — Verse (Arrival)**

* Only the verse is visible
* Calm, uncluttered

---

**Step 2 — Reflection**
Appears when the user:

* taps “Begin”
* taps “Listen”
* pauses briefly (~2–3 seconds)

👉 Not forced — simply *invited*

---

**Step 3 — Prayer**
Appears:

* after reflection completes
  OR
* after user engages (reply, scroll, listen)

---

**Step 4 — Understand / Sermon**
Appears:

* after prayer moment settles
* not immediately stacked

---

### **Key Mechanic:**

* Use **soft transitions** (fade, slide, expand)
* Avoid hard “step changes”

---

### **Big Risk to Avoid:**

If users feel:

> “I have to complete this to move on”

You lose:

* trust
* calm
* authenticity

---

### **Core Principle:**

> Guide the user forward — never control their path

---

---

### **Question 4 — Retention**

**Problem:**
You currently rely on:

* streaks
* notifications
* reminders

These create:
👉 **pressure, not desire**

---

### **What actually brings users back:**

Not:

> “I need to keep my streak”

But:

> **“That helped me… I want that again.”**

---

### **Retention is driven by:**

#### 1. Emotional Continuity

User feels:

* “This knows where I left off”

---

#### 2. Personal Relevance

User feels:

* “This is for me, today”

---

#### 3. Gentle Familiarity

User feels:

* “I know what I’ll find here”

---

---

### **What Shepherd’s Path should feel like on re-entry:**

Not:

> “Here’s today’s content”

But:

> **“Welcome back — let’s keep walking”**

---

### **Subtle retention drivers:**

* Light acknowledgment of prior day:

  > “Yesterday you reflected on…” (optional, not heavy)

* Consistent structure (familiar rhythm)

* No pressure language:

  * No guilt
  * No “you missed a day” tone

---

### **Core Principle:**

> People return to places that felt meaningful — not places that reminded them to return

---

---

### **Question 5 — Pricing Page**

**Current issue:**
Feature list = transactional
Your app = relational

---

### **What it SHOULD communicate:**

Not:

> “Here’s what you get”

But:

> **“Here’s what this becomes”**

---

### **The ONE thing it must communicate:**

> **Pro is how you go deeper in something that already matters to you**

---

### **The ONE thing it must avoid:**

* Feature-heavy explanation
* Technical breakdowns
* Anything that feels like selling

---

---

### **Recommended Structure:**

**1. Opening (Emotional Anchor)**

> “You’ve already started something here…”
>
> “Pro simply takes you deeper.”

---

**2. What “Deeper” Means (Not Features — Experience)**

* Deeper understanding of Scripture
* More personalized guidance
* Continued daily messages that meet you where you are

---

**3. Gentle Clarity (Light Feature Framing)**

* Daily context
* Daily message (sermon)
* Unlimited guidance

(keep minimal, no overload)

---

**4. Invitation (Not Conversion)**

👉 “Continue deeper”
👉 “Stay on the path”

NOT:

* “Upgrade now”
* “Unlock features”

---

---

### **Core Principle:**

> The pricing page should feel like an invitation into depth — not a transaction

---

---

## **Final Summary**

**Progressive Reveal:**

* Let the experience unfold naturally
* No forced steps, no gating flow

---

**Retention:**

* Driven by meaning, not mechanics
* Users return because it mattered, not because they’re reminded

---

**Pricing:**

* Sell depth, not features
* Invite, don’t push

---

## **End Goal**

* The app feels guided, not controlled
* The user returns because they want to
* Pro feels like a natural next step, not a paywall
