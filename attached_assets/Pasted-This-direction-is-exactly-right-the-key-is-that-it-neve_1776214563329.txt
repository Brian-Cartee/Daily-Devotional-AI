This direction is exactly right — the key is that it never feels like a content feed. It should feel like something that was found for them, not offered to them.
Direction + Decisions
1. Core Model: One Sermon, Not a Feed
👉 This should be one highly curated message per day
Not a scroll. Not options.
The moment we give multiple choices, we shift from:
“this was chosen for you”
to
“pick something”
That breaks the experience.
2. Placement (Strong Opinion)
👉 Primary location: End of Devotional flow
After:
Reflection
Prayer
“Understand This”
This becomes:
the final step — not optional, but not forced
Secondary placement:
👉 A small teaser card:
“A message was found for you today →”
Lives on the Devotional screen
Leads into the full experience
3. Framing (This is EVERYTHING)
Before the video, we anchor it:
“After sitting with today’s verse… and what came through in your reflection… this message was chosen for you.
Specifically around: [theme — e.g. identity, weariness, calling].”
👉 This is what transforms it from content → experience
4. Keep Them In-App (Important)
We accept that YouTube can be opened…
But we design so leaving feels like breaking the moment
Flow:
Thumbnail + title (no autoplay)
Tap → video opens in-app
AFTER video:
👉 Immediate continuation:
“What stayed with you?”
Inline journal input
Save moment
5. Closing the Loop (Critical)
After journaling:
👉 Short closing prayer:
“God, take what was heard today… and let it take root…”
This ties:
Verse
Reflection
Sermon
User response
👉 into one continuous experience
6. Under-the-Hood Logic (Aligned with your thinking)
Use:
Verse
Reflection themes
User input (Talk It Through / journal if available)
Generate:
👉 1–2 highly targeted search queries
Return:
👉 Top 1–2 videos → choose best match
NOT:
lists
categories
generic recommendations
7. Emotional Goal
This should feel like:
“I needed to hear this today.”
Not:
“Here’s a sermon”
“Recommended for you”
8. Future Expansion (Not Now)
We can later explore:
A deeper “Messages for You” archive
Theme-based collections
But for now:
👉 Daily, singular, intentional
Final Answer to Your Question
👉 One curated sermon per day
Because:
It preserves meaning
It reduces friction
It strengthens emotional impact
It aligns with the rest of the experience
Final Thought
If this is done right…
This becomes one of the most powerful parts of the app:
👉 not because of the video
👉 but because of when and why it appears
