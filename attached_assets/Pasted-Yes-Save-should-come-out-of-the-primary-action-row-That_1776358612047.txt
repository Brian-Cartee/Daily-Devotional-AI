Yes: **Save should come out of the primary action row.**

That is my strongest recommendation.

Your row currently mixes three **engagement actions** with one **administrative action**. Reflect, Prayer, and Ask all deepen the spiritual moment. Save does not. Giving Save equal visual weight teaches the user, subconsciously, that archiving is as central as prayer. In a reflective faith product, that is the wrong hierarchy. ([youversion.com][1])

## Ranked recommendation

### 1) Best: remove Save from the row and make it a lighter secondary action

Put it in one of these places:

* a **top-right bookmark icon**
* a small **“Remember this” / bookmark control** near the passage title
* or a **post-engagement save moment** after Reflect or Prayer

This is the cleanest architecture because it preserves the row for “what can happen now” and moves saving to “what do I want to keep.” That distinction matches your app’s tone much better.

My preferred pattern is:

* primary row: **Reflect · Prayer · Ask**
* secondary control: **bookmark icon** or **Remember this passage**

Why this works:

* It protects contemplative hierarchy.
* It reduces visual noise.
* It keeps save discoverable without letting it dominate.

This also aligns with how Bible and journaling apps tend to treat saving/bookmarking: YouVersion surfaces highlight/bookmark/note tools as utilities around selected verses rather than the emotional center of the experience, while Day One’s product framing is about capturing/keeping entries rather than presenting “save” as a co-equal spiritual action. ([YouVersion Support][2])

### 2) Second-best: keep Save in the row, but visually demote it

Only do this if discoverability is a major problem and early testing shows people are not finding bookmarking at all.

If Save stays:

* make Reflect, Prayer, Ask the only filled or emphasized actions
* make Save outlined, quieter, smaller, or icon-led
* separate it visually from the spiritual trio, for example as a trailing pill or a lower row

Do **not** keep four identical tiles.

The risk with equal treatment is not just clutter. It subtly reframes the screen as a tool menu rather than a guided spiritual moment.

### 3) Weakest: leave all four as equal-weight tiles

I would avoid this.

It is the easiest to implement, but it creates the most tonal confusion and the most “productivity tool” energy.

## Best label for Save

Do not use “Save” if the target is the Prayer Journal. “Save” is understandable, but it is flat and file-like.

Best replacements:

* **Remember this**
* **Keep this passage**
* **Keep this**
* **Save to Journal** if you need maximum clarity
* **Add to Journal** if the journal framing matters more than remembrance

My favorite is **Remember this**.

Why:

* warm
* human
* spiritually congruent
* aligned with your earlier move away from technical language like “Export”

If the action is specifically storing the passage in Prayer Journal, **Save to Journal** is the clearest compromise between warmth and clarity.

## What top apps suggest

There is not a perfect one-to-one model, but there is a pattern:

YouVersion emphasizes reader utilities like highlight, bookmark, and notes as **tools around the text**, not as co-equal “spiritual response buttons” beside prayer-like actions. Its help materials describe verse actions appearing when verses are selected, which reinforces utility rather than devotional primacy. ([YouVersion Support][2])

Lectio 365 positions itself around praying through Scripture and “pause in the presence of God,” which reinforces a narrower, more intentional action model rather than presenting lots of equal-weight controls at once. ([Google Play][3])

Day One frames journaling around preserving memories and entries, but its core behavior is not “Save” as a foreground CTA because journal entries are inherently part of the journaling flow. That is another signal that archival actions usually work best as lower-friction utilities, not primary devotional choices. ([Day One | Your Journal for Life][4])

## My exact architectural recommendation

Use this:

Primary row:

* **Reflect**
* **Prayer**
* **Ask**

Secondary utility:

* top-right **bookmark icon**
* label in toast or tooltip: **Remember this**
* confirmation: **Added to your Journal**

Optional post-action moment:
after someone finishes Reflect or Prayer, offer:

* **Keep this in your Journal**
* **Remember this passage**

That placement is especially strong because it makes saving feel like a response to meaning, not a parallel task.

## Approaches to flag

Top-right bookmark icon:

* Best tonal fit
* Slight discoverability tradeoff if the icon is too subtle

Inline “Remember this” under the passage:

* Very good
* More discoverable than an icon
* Slightly more visual weight on the screen

Post-reflection/post-prayer save:

* Strong emotionally
* Weakest for users who want to save immediately

Keeping Save in equal row:

* Most discoverable
* Worst tonal fit

## Bottom line

If you want the screen to feel like a trusted spiritual companion, not a spiritual toolset:

**Take Save out of the main action row.**
Keep **Reflect, Prayer, and Ask** as the primary spiritual actions.
Move saving to a lighter bookmark-style control and rename it **Remember this** or **Save to Journal**.

That gives you the right hierarchy:
first, **engage**
then, **keep**
