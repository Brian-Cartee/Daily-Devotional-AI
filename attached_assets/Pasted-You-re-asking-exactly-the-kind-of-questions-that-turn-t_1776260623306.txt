We are refining UX for clarity, consistency, and user trust. Please make the following improvements:

### 1. Footer Consistency

Issue:
The footer currently mixes styles (some items have emojis, some are colored, some are plain), which creates visual inconsistency.

Changes:

* Remove all emojis from footer items
* Create a clear hierarchy:

  * Primary items (e.g., “Beginning with Jesus”, “The Greatest Gift”) → keep accent color
  * Secondary items → standard white text
  * Utility items (Privacy Policy, Terms, Contact) → slightly reduced opacity

Goal:
Make the footer feel clean, calm, and intentional — not visually noisy.

---

### 2. “Your Iron Circle” Section

Issue:
This section is visually dominant but is not the primary daily action, creating competition with more important elements.

Changes:

* Slightly reduce visual intensity (less glow, slightly flatter gradient, or reduced height)
* Maintain design but make it feel secondary to core daily actions

Goal:
Ensure the most visually dominant elements reflect the most important user actions.

---

### 3. Reading Plans Navigation Behavior

Issue:
Tapping “Reading Plans” currently lands the user mid-page, which feels confusing and unpredictable.

Changes:

* Always scroll to the top of the Reading Plans section when accessed from navigation
* Do NOT auto-scroll to a previously selected plan
* If a user has an active plan, highlight it clearly within the section (e.g., “Continue your plan”)

Goal:
Navigation should feel predictable and intuitive. The user should always know where they are and why.

---

Do not redesign layouts. Focus only on clarity, hierarchy, and predictability.
