We are refining the UI of Shepherd’s Path to maintain a calm, distraction-free, non-app-like experience.

Context:
Shepherd’s Path is a quiet, consistent presence — not a traditional app. Every screen should feel like a place someone arrives, not a tool they operate.

---

## NAVIGATION RULES (IMPORTANT)

* Do NOT add back arrows to any top-level tab screens
* The bottom navigation bar is the only navigation system at the root level
* Back arrows should ONLY appear on deeper, drill-down screens (e.g., sessions, results, detail views)

---

## SCREEN-SPECIFIC REFINEMENTS

### 1. Explore Scripture (Scripture tab)

* Keep title: “Explore Scripture”
* Update subtitle to:
  “Bring a question, a passage, or what’s on your mind.”
* Maintain clean spacing and hierarchy
* Do not add additional UI elements to the hero

---

### 2. Stories / Community Screen

* Keep headline but consider slightly grounding tone if needed
* Reduce prominence of floating “Share your story” button:

  * Slightly smaller OR
  * Slight opacity reduction OR
  * Delay appearance until scroll

Goal: reduce social-media feeling and maintain calm tone

---

### 3. Journal Screen

* Keep marble background (this is a strong visual anchor)
* Update subtitle to one of:

  * “A place to remember your walk with Jesus.”
  * OR “A place to hold what you’ve walked through.”
* Reduce visual weight of “Export” button:

  * Lower contrast OR
  * Move slightly out of primary visual focus

Goal: prioritize emotional tone over utility

---

### 4. Journeys Screen

* Keep current path image (this is correct direction)
* Keep headline: “Start where you are”
* Keep subtext: “There’s a path for this moment.”
* Simplify or remove “BIBLE JOURNEYS” label:

  * Option: shorten to “Journeys”
  * OR remove entirely

Goal: reduce system/category feel and increase emotional clarity

---

## GLOBAL DESIGN PRINCIPLE

Before adding any UI element, ask:

“Does this make the screen feel more like a tool… or more like a place?”

If it feels like a tool, remove or simplify it.

We are optimizing for:

* calm
* clarity
* emotional resonance
* minimal cognitive load

Not:

* feature visibility
* navigation density
* or app-like structure

---

Proceed with these refinements while preserving current layout and spacing system.
