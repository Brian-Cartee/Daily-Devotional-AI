Direction + Decisions
1. Where it lives
This should not live in a traditional help location (like a ? icon or separate help page).
Instead:
👉 It should live inside the experience, not outside of it.
Accessible from the “For You” screen
Potentially introduced subtly on first visit
Can be revisited via a small, non-intrusive entry point (not a primary nav item)
Goal:
👉 It should feel like guidance, not support documentation
2. Structure (Steps)
Your instinct is right on keeping it short and focused.
Let’s define it as:
“A Simple Walkthrough” (4–5 short moments)
Talk It Through
This is where everything begins
Share what you’re facing — no pressure, no perfect words needed
How the Response Works
The app responds personally using scripture, reflection, and guidance
It’s built around what the user shares
Your Journey
The app can create a Bible-based path forward
Not just a one-time response, but something ongoing
Listen Feature
Everything can be listened to
Designed to feel like someone speaking directly to you
Journal / Save
Users can keep what matters
Track what they’re going through over time
(We can lightly mention Pro at the end, but it should not interrupt the flow)
3. Scripted vs AI Agent
👉 Go scripted walkthrough for this
Reason:
Faster
More consistent
Keeps tone controlled and intentional
Avoids unnecessary complexity and cost
This is not meant to be a conversation — it’s a guided introduction
Additional Direction (Important)
This should feel like:
Calm
Personal
Reassuring
NOT:
Instructional
Over-explained
Technical
Tone Guidance
Think:
“Let me show you how this works”
Not:
“Here’s how to use the app”
