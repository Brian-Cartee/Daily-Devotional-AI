## **Shepherd’s Path — Product Direction (Q15–Q17)**

---

### **Question 15 — The Journal**

**Core truth:**
Journaling doesn’t stick because of discipline.
It sticks when it feels **personally meaningful and spiritually alive**.

---

### **What the journal is NOT:**

* Not a log
* Not a productivity habit
* Not something to “keep up with”

---

### **What it SHOULD be:**

> **A quiet place where someone notices what God is doing in their life**

---

### **The true purpose:**

* A **record of movement** (not entries)
* A **place to return to meaning**
* A **mirror of growth over time**

---

### **What makes journaling stick:**

#### 1. It’s optional, not expected

No pressure to write daily
No guilt when they don’t

---

#### 2. It’s anchored in moments

Users don’t “go journal”

👉 They:

* save a prayer
* write after something meaningful
* respond to a moment

---

#### 3. It feels worth returning to

> “I want to remember this”

---

---

### **How AI should interact with the journal:**

**Must feel:**

* respectful
* quiet
* background-aware

---

### **What it should NOT feel like:**

* monitored
* analyzed
* quoted back too directly

---

### **Best approach:**

Use journal content to:

* shape tone
* understand themes
* deepen relevance

---

Avoid:

* repeating exact phrases
* referencing entries explicitly unless necessary

---

### **Example tone:**

Not:

> “You wrote last week that you felt…”

But:

> “It sounds like this has been something you’ve been carrying for a while…”

---

---

### **Core Principle:**

> The journal is sacred space — the AI is a quiet listener, not a commentator

---

---

### **Question 16 — The Seeker**

**Core truth:**
You are not guiding them to belief.

👉 You are **meeting them in honesty**

---

### **What the seeker feels:**

* Unsure
* Curious
* Possibly resistant
* Often hurting

---

### **What the app must NOT do:**

* Do not persuade
* Do not “close”
* Do not assume belief

---

---

### **What the experience SHOULD feel like:**

> **“I can be here without pretending.”**

---

---

### **What the seeker most needs:**

#### 1. Safety

No expectations
No pressure

---

#### 2. Honesty

Language that allows:

* doubt
* questions
* uncertainty

---

#### 3. Presence

Not answers
Not arguments

👉 Just:

> calm, grounded guidance

---

---

### **How Christianity should show up:**

* Clear, but not forceful
* Present, but not persuasive
* Lived, not explained

---

---

### **Example posture:**

Not:

> “Here’s the truth you need to accept”

But:

> “Here’s what this says — you can sit with it”

---

---

### **Core Principle:**

> Let them encounter truth — don’t try to convince them of it

---

---

### **Question 17 — The Daily Verse**

**Core truth:**
This is one of your most important decisions.

---

### **The power of the current model:**

One shared verse per day

👉 This creates:

* unity
* rhythm
* sense of receiving

---

---

### **Why this matters deeply:**

If users choose their verse:

* it becomes preference

If users receive the verse:

* it becomes formation

---

---

### **Recommendation (Strong):**

👉 **Keep one shared daily verse**

---

---

### **Should users ever have agency?**

Yes — but not in the core flow.

---

### **Allow choice only outside the main experience:**

* Explore
* Scripture tab

---

👉 But never replace:

> the daily received word

---

---

### **Should the verse be personalized?**

No — not the primary one.

---

Because:

* personalization follows the user
* Scripture should sometimes **lead the user**

---

---

### **What makes this powerful:**

The moment when:

> “I didn’t choose this… but I needed it”

---

---

### **Core Principle:**

> The daily verse is received, not selected

---

---

## **Final Summary**

**Journal:**

* A sacred, optional space
* Built around meaning, not habit
* AI listens quietly, never intrusively

---

**Seeker:**

* Must feel safe, not persuaded
* Christianity is present, not pushed

---

**Daily Verse:**

* Remains shared and received
* Forms the user, not the other way around

---

## **End Goal**

* The journal becomes a place of reflection, not obligation
* The seeker feels welcomed, not targeted
* The verse remains a steady anchor — not a customizable feed
